# AI Acceptable Use Guide
**Instructor:** Dr. Jim D. Pham  
**Course:** All Programming Classes  
**Effective Year:** 2026  

---

## 💡 Approved AI Applications (Acceptable Uses)

Students are encouraged to responsibly integrate AI tools into their daily learning and development workflows for the following tasks:

* **Conceptual Clarification:** You are encouraged to leverage AI to obtain thorough explanations, clear examples, and concise summaries of complex computer science and programming topics to enhance your foundational understanding.
* **Algorithmic Guidance & Hint Generation:** AI may be used as an educational guide to offer high-level logic, architectural hints, or structural strategies. This helps you think critically about your assignments and promotes independent problem-solving without delivering verbatim answers.
* **Code Debugging & Troubleshooting:** AI serves as a powerful resource for parsing compiler errors, isolating bugs, and troubleshooting unexpected code execution. Utilizing AI in this manner helps refine your debugging skills and optimizes your runtime logic.

---

## 🛠️ Suggested Technical Platforms

You are free to utilize the AI ecosystem that best fits your development preferences. Recommended conversational and search tools include:

* **General Conversational Models:** ChatGPT (OpenAI), Claude AI (Anthropic), Gemini (Google)
* **Development & Computational Engines:** Copilot (Microsoft), DeepSeek
* **Research & Synthesis Discovery:** Perplexity
* **Alternative Tools:** Other equivalent LLM or developer-focused AI assistance software.

---

## 🚫 Strictly Prohibited Actions (Unacceptable Uses)

To maintain academic rigor and preserve your personal skill growth, the following actions constitute academic dishonesty:

* **Plagiarism & Verbatim Submissions:** Copying, pasting, or submitting AI-generated code blocks directly into your lab assignments, projects, or worksheets without independent comprehension or authorization is strictly prohibited. All final source code must reflect your own engineering capability.
* **Examination & Assessment Interference:** Utilizing AI assistants, extensions, or browser tools during Chapter Quizzes, the Midterm Exam, or the Final Exam is an explicit violation of academic integrity. All formal assessments must be completed purely as your own unassisted individual work.

---

## 🛑 Failure to Comply
Violations of these guidelines undermine the learning process and are treated as infractions of institutional academic integrity policies. Your work should reflect your personal intellectual journey and technical growth.
